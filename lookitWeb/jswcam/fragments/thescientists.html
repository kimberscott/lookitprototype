Meet the scientists!

Kim Scott

Kim is a graduate student in the Early Childhood Cognition Lab and Mama to four-and-a-half ("and two quarter inches!") year old Remy. She developed Lookit partly to enable other single parents to participate in research!

Research interests: Origins of conscious experience--or what it's like to be a baby

Hobbies: Salsa dancing and bicycling

Laura Schulz

Laura is the PI of the Early Childhood Cognition Lab.

Research interests: How children arrive at a common-sense understanding of the physical and social world through exploration and instruction.

Hobbies: hiking, reading, and playing

Jean Chow

Jean is a junior at MIT majoring in Brain & Cognitive Sciences. She answers questions from Lookit participants and codes the videos you send in, recording children's answers or looking time.

Research interests: cognitive development and learning in young children

Hobbies: Running, cycling, Taekwondo, art, being outdoors, chilling with her dog

Melissa Kline

Melissa is a graduate student in the Early Childhood Cognition Lab. She advised and designed stimuli for the "Learning New Verbs" study.

Research interests: Interfaces between language and cognition - how young children match up complex sentences with a complex world, and how they use what they know about people to learn language.

Hobbies: Sewing, tango, and singing.

Tomer Ullman

Tomer is a graduate student working in collaboration with the Early Childhood Cognition Lab, father of 2-year-old Gabriel.

Research interests: How babies and children learn intuitive physics and intuitive psychology

Hobbies: Running and board games

Rachel Magid

Rachel is the lab coordinator for the ECCL and is happy to answer questions and hear about your experience participating in online research studies on Lookit.

Research interests: Learning and imagination--how do our goals help us learn?

Hobbies: Reading historical fiction and cooking

Julian Jara-Ettinger

Julian is a third-year graduate student in the Early Childhood Cognition Lab.

Research interests: The development of our commonsense psychology. What do children understand about minds?

Hobbies: Building stuff with legos and playing music.

Hilary Richardson

Hilary is a graduate student in the Saxe lab at MIT, which works to understand how we think about other people. Since she's interested primarily in how the ability to understand other people develops, she works mostly with kids, and hangs around the Early Childhood Cognition Lab quite a bit!

Research interests: Understanding how young children and babies think about other people.

Hobbies: Spending time outside and Zumba

Hyowon Gweon

Hyo is a post-doc in the Early Childhood Cognition Lab!

Research interests: Children's inferences and social evaluations in communicative contexts: how do children learn from information provided by others?

Hobbies: Making toys for her experiments

Tobi Gerstenberg

Tobi is a postdoc in Prof Josh Tenenbaum's Computational Cognitive Science Lab and also works with Prof Laura Schulz and the members of the Early Childhood Cognition Lab.

Research interests: Causal judgments, attributions of responsibility, and counterfactual thinking.

Hobbies: Playing guitar, many (if not most) ball sports.
